RED

RED involves research teams in 4 countries:

  • Germany, led by Felicitas Macgilchrist
  • Sweden, led by Thomas Hillman
  • Mexico, led by Inés Dussel
  • South Africa, led by Paul Prinsloo

The project investigates how schooling is being reconfigured through digital technology in different regions of the world. It asks in what ways are these changes are exacerbating, reproducing or creating new forms of inequality and/or promoting equality?

With field work in Sweden, Germany, Mexico, Argentina, South Africa, and Botswana, RED investigates:

  1. Policy: How do policies and data infrastructures for digital education address inequality?
  2. Practice: How do selected privileged and disadvantaged schools engage with digital technology and thus (re)create or contest inequality?
  3. Profiles: How are schools’ ‘data profiles’ entangled with in/equality?

RED orients to vital, resource and existential in/equality. It prioritises the politics of technology and decolonial thinking. It utilizes established ethnographic methods and novel data audit and digital methods to craft powerful narratives.

It aims to:

  1. map global phenomena and local specificities
  2. develop contextualised understandings of technology use in dis/advantaged schools
  3. generate profiles of how – and by whom – student data are colonised across global contexts
  4. problematize simplistic accounts of how technology can alleviate inequalities
  5. identify mechanisms through which technology and/or data sovereignty do promote equality

Overall, RED contributes a global perspective on technology, education and society. It also provides actionable insights for designing and regulating the use of technology in education to shape a more equitable future.
